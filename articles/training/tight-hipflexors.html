As triathletes, we are going to have tight hip flexors after the IM bike, because our hip flexors are tight, it prohibits the amount of extension we can get. A typical runner wants good extension out the back of a running stride, extension is created through the glutes and hamstrings. We are not typical runners. So the long stride / slow cadence just wonâ€™t work in Ironman running. Overstriding by default results in more impact per strike which in turn break the muscle tissue down more.

We incorporate daily mobility work each morning that opens up our hip flexors, this will help and is needed. Continuing our mobility work and focusing in our running on a shorter snappier stride allows for your foot-strike to happen underneath your hips, you are recruiting more fatigue resistant muscles (glutes and hamstrings) and opposed to quads (which have more fast twitch fibers and fatigue easily)

We will be doing lots of neuro + strength work over the off season where high cadence is a focus on the run and low cadence on the bike to maintain strength. We are also trying to nail the daily mobility routine which will help opening up our hip flexors (also, paying special attention to the psoas video foam rolling video, this helps with our hip mobility and under-active glutes).
