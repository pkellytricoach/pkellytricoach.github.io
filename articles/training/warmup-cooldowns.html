The importance of warm ups and cool downs

We normally do 15/20 mins of warm up/cool downs in our sessions. Why?

Warm up An easy warm up increases blood flow to the muscles, which enhances the delivery of oxygen and nutrients to our cells. This allows the removal of waste products. This is why sometimes when we start our exercise we feel sluggish, but after 20 mins we come to life. At least this is the case for me. So always give at least 20 mins of your workout. If you are still feeling crap after 20 mins, then something is up with your body. Back off and have an easy session of finish there and then and come back stronger tomorrow.

Cool down Our bodies are designed to move. A cooldown after a race or hard training effort is especially important. Effort should be easy. The key is simply to pump nutrient dense blood from your heart through your arteries to all your different cells. That same blood picks up metabolic waste and removes it through our kidneys via our veins. This whole process is facilitated by movement. The goal of these cool downs is never to gain fitness, more to expedite recovery.
